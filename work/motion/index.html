08 — sticky stacking cards

How we
build motion

A

Storyboard the feeling

Before a single keyframe, we name the emotion each interaction should leave behind. A press should feel decisive. A reveal should feel earned. We sketch timing charts on paper, like animators do.

B

Prototype in the browser

Motion can't be approved in a static comp. We build the real thing early — transform and opacity only, compositor-friendly, measured against a 60fps budget on mid-range hardware, not our own machines.

C

Tune the easing

The difference between "fine" and "expensive" is the curve. We hand-tune every cubic-bezier and lerp factor until the motion has weight — then we cut 20% of the duration, because confidence is fast.

D

Respect the reader

Every effect ships with an exit: reduced-motion users get a calm, fully readable page; touch devices get native scrolling; keyboards get visible focus. Motion is a layer, never a wall.
